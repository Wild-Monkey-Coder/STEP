About Me

I was born in the US and was raised here in Georgia until I was 11 years old. My parents are from Colombia, but in 2011 they moved back and I went with them. I lived there for 6 years in the city of Bogota before moving back to the US with my great aunt so I could finish high school, find better opportunities to succeed professionally, and attend a better university than I would have been able to afford in Colombia.
From a young age I was interested in technology and knew I wanted to learn to create amazing products. At that time, I thought I would grow up to become an engineer. It wasn't until high school that I learned about computer science and programming, and soon I knew that I wanted to have a career in software. This interest in the field that pushed me to be part of CSSI in 2019, and return to Google as a STEP intern in 2020. Currently, I am a Computer Science student at the University of Gerogia with a minor in Cognitive Science.
In my free time, I enjoy watching super hero movies and playing vdeo games. I especially enjoy stategy games or base building games where I get to find creative solutions to problems and can help me keep my critical thinking skills sharp even when I'm winding down. However, entertainment has also been a source of inspiration for me. Science fiction and fantasy allow us to imagine what the world could be like, and often inspire me to contemplate how possible some fictional technologies.

Interests

I have been playing league of legends since 2015 when my best friend introduced me to the game. I didn't play often because back then my computer was so bad we called it a toaster. When I moved back to the US, I started working part time and bought a new computer which could run the game. I still play the game at least once a week because it's how I stay in touch with my best friend who is still in Colombia. Personally, I enjoy the game because of the high skill ceiling many of the champions have which offer interesting goals to work towards. However, I sometimes don't enjoy playing because of how the game evolves over time, requiring that I invest more time (which I don't have much of these days) to adapt to the changing meta.

Besides MOBA games like League of legends, I also really enojy base-building and strategy games like Oxygen Not Included. What's great about this game is that it pushes me to try and build a colony while trying to overcome multiple issues that can come up, included the one presented in the title: a lack of oxygen for colonists. I've played this game and lost track of time trying to design efficient base set ups and trying to conqueor the asteroid, and the underlying mechanics which govern the game such as power production, heat, disease, automation, etc., make it an amazing game I love to play.

I have always wanted to work on creating new technologies which would change the world for the better. Something that excites is the idea of space colonization and human expansion. In recent years, governments and people have begun thinking about colonizing Mars, which is a remarkable endeavor. Though I am interested because of my love of making science fiction a reality, the idea of colonizing Mars is also motivated by the fact that in a few years we may finish destroying the Earth, and to give our species and descendents hope, humanity needs a backup planet. I am frightened that my grandchildren will inherit an inhospitable planet, so the thought that I could contribute to giving them a fresh start on another world consoles me.

I am a Computer Science major, but what this means for me is that I am learning and acquiring the tools to work in almost any field I choose. For a long time I didn't know what I was really interested in, but a few years ago I realized that what really interested me was artificial intelligence. What I truly want to do in the future is conduct research on human-computer interaction and digital assitants, and make AI more accesible to individuals. When I envision the future, I want to help build a future where software doesn't replace people, but instead empowers them to do greater things they couldn't have before.
